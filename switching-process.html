Switching Process

As part of the switching process, we validate as much of the customer input data as possible to ensure that by the time we pass the data onto you, it’s as comprehensive and accurate as it can be. To provide a perfect switch we have presented a list of guidelines that you may want to take into account, which have been listed below.

MPAS Information

Process

  • Systems automatically identify MPAS information*
  • MPAS, MPRNs and meter serial numbers are all included
  • MPAS information can be sent on to you

*approximately 92% of cases MPAS is identified

Options

If no information is found, either:

  • Accept and process the customers information automatically
  • Ask the customer to provide their details manually

Credit Check

Process

  • Integration of third party credit check
  • Permission from customer required

Options

In some cases, the customer may not want to go through a credit check process and so it’s up to you to decide if they are allowed to progress with your specific tariff.

Address

Process

One of the options you get as the supplier is to input clear specifications that you require the customer to meet in order to allow them to proceed with your tariff. For choosing the address requirements, many different suppliers have different preferences.

Options

  • All addresses in the previous three years
  • 3 addresses in the previous three years
  • Accept or reject customers with a previous overseas address if this directly before existing address.

Age

Process

State the age range of customers you are happy with taking a tariff, meaning that you will get more specific viewers to these supply options.

Options

Majority of suppliers currently allow their services for customers between the ages of 18-110, although you can set an age range to whatever you desire.

Type of switch

Process

Since we offer online as well as offline support, you Even get the choice of how your customers make the final switch to your service.

Options

  • Online switches only
  • Online quote with offline confirmation
  • Offline switch via inbound call
  • Offline switch via outbound call
  • Face to face switch
  • Automated switch with LOA (Letter of Authority)

Closure tariff runtime

Process

In some circumstances the customer may have not completed the switch by the time you have removed the tariff from sale. So, in order for you not to lose out on a sale or get out of date applications, we allow you to choose how long your tariff stays up for.

Options

  • Customer will be accepted up to xx days after the tariff is closed for sale so long as they had requested but not fully completed the switch at the time due to missing data.
  • Customer only accepted up to the end of the day that the tariff ended so long as switch data provided to you within standard timeline.
  • Customer only accepted up to the exact time that the tariff ended so long as the switch data provided to you within a standard timeline.
